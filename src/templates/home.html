Crime in Austin

This Crime Analysis seeks to delineate a pattern between Crime Data in Austin from 2020-2021, TX zip codes and several different segments within each zip code such as; median household income, food scarcity, daily weather events, and time frames based on monthly and hourly increments. The data pipeline uses Python libraries for ETL into the visualization deployment. Tableau and Unsupervised Machine Learning is implemented to deploy visualizations of trends, if any, between crime and other data points.

The chart below shows the total count of crimes committed in Greater Austin area Zip-codes and each crime was categorized as violent or nonviolent crimes based on the criteria provided by the FBI’s UCR (Universal Crime Reporting) website. This categorization for each crime was achieved by using Python scripts to scan each of the 313 crimes for key words that pertained to those violent crimes, and would assign them ‘V’ for violent crimes and conversely ‘NV’ for nonviolent crimes. The result of the analysis showed that there were significant differences between the amount of crimes committed in these zip codes and that the results suggest that those with lower incomes experienced higher total crime counts than zip codes with higher average incomes. The data also showed that there were significantly more nonviolent crimes than violent crimes in general.

Crime by Time

The first goal of the time analysis was to determine when crimes occur in Austin based on hour intervals throughout the day. In order to look at the total crimes that occured within a 24 hour timeframe in the City of Austin, code was formulated using python's groupby function and pythons datetime converter to group crimes count by each hour (12:00 refers to the time interval 12:00-12:59). The time formatted for based on military time. It was determined that most crimes in the austin area occured between the hours of midnight (00:00) and noon (12:00). The times in which crimes showed a massive decline in frequency was bewteen the times of 00:00 to 8:00, while a consistent rise in crimes occured past 13:00 until 18:00 where they hit a 3rd peak. The average amount of crimes that happen between each out amounted to 7779 crimes per hour. This was consistent throughout all zipcodes.

Crime by Month

The analysis of Austin crimes that occured over the 24 month period was accomplished similarly to that of the crime by hour analysis. Pythons groupby function was used for months instead of time and crime severity, and what was discovered was that overall reported crime frequency throughout 2020-2021 crime activity of violent and nonviolent crimes slightly decreased overall throughout the years. The maximum amount of reported crimes that occured happened on January 2020 with 7519 nonviolent crimes and 1113 violent crimes. Nonviolent crimes made up the majority of crimes during all the months. However, higher frequencies of violent crimes were seen during the hotter times of the year, such as May, June, July, and August. There is a significant drop in total crimes in Febuary 2021 and one factor to consider was that the freeze of 2021 affect crimes and the ability to report those crimes.

Additional Charts were created to help visualize crime counts by month and crime severity. To the right, density bar charts were created using a color scale based on the count of crimes that were commited with each block being the month those crimes occured in. It can be seen that violent crime are more likely to happen during the summer months.
